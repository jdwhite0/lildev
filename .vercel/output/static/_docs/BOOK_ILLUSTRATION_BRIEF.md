## THE PROJECT

**Three original picture books** for the Lil Dev World franchise.

Each book is independently complete. They do not need to be read in sequence. They share a visual world, the same characters, and the same emotional tone — but each has its own story and its own emotional question.

**The books are:**

| # | Title | Protagonist | Central Theme |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | *Silent Prayer* | David Sanders (Lil Dev) | What if I can't find the words to pray? |
| 2 | *Mirror Reflections* | Max (David's best friend) | What if I keep comparing myself to others? |
| 3 | *Love Multiplied* | Lily (David's friend) | How do I help someone whose hurt is bigger than my words? |

**Commission order (recommended):**
Start with *Love Multiplied* — it has the warmest, clearest brief and is the best project to establish the visual standard for all three books. Then *Silent Prayer*, then *Mirror Reflections*.

However, for the launch, *Silent Prayer* (David's book) is the flagship and must ship first. Plan accordingly with your illustrator.

## ART STYLE — NON-NEGOTIABLE

The visual standard across all three books must be identical. Every book exists in the same world.

### The Style

**2D flat cel illustration — bold outlines, flat color, atmospheric lighting.**

Specifically:
- **Bold, consistent outlines** on all characters — they read clearly against any background
- **Flat color fills on characters** — no volume shading or rendering on the characters themselves
- **Atmospheric lighting in environments** — warmth comes from light overlays (lamp pools, window beams, moonlight), not from shading the surfaces
- **Warm, muted palette** — soft ambers, warm tans, muted greens, deep blues for night scenes; nothing neon or oversaturated

### The Palette by Book

| Book | Primary Palette | Key Light Sources |
|---|---|---|
| *Silent Prayer* | Deep blue (night sky), warm amber (nightlight), white-blue (moonlight) | Bedroom nightlight, window moonlight, Atlanta streetlights |
| *Love Multiplied* | Warm golden afternoon, classroom neutrals, sunset oranges | Afternoon sunlight, warm interior lamps, sunset from windows |
| *Mirror Reflections* | Variable — bedroom blues, school warm tones, park greens | Morning light, fluorescent classroom, open sky |

### Style References

Study these for tone, line quality, and emotional register:

- **Ezra Jack Keats** (*The Snowy Day*) — simple shapes, warm palette, powerful emotional presence
- **Kadir Nelson** (*He's Got the Whole World in His Hands*, *We Are the Ship*) — rich color, dignified Black characters, warmth
- **Jerry Pinkney** (any work) — warmth, emotional depth, Black American life
- **Vashti Harrison** (*Little Leaders*) — clean lines, flat color, modern children's illustration

Do NOT reference:
- Cartoon network or anime styles
- Overly cute/chibi proportions
- Photo-realistic illustration
- Coloring-book thin line weights

## CHARACTER CONSISTENCY RULES

This is the most important technical requirement of the entire job.

Every illustration of David Sanders must match the approved character sheets exactly:
- Same skin tone (deep, rich warm brown — never lightened or altered)
- Same hair (tight natural crop, fade at hairline, visible at temples)
- Same proportions and build
- Same cap (backward, brand blue #3B64B6)
- Expressions must match the approved expression sheet

Every illustration of Angel must match exactly:
- All-white fur, flat cel shading, bold outline
- Same size relative to David (knee-height or lower)
- Same warm dark eyes and black button nose

**If the illustrator cannot maintain character consistency across 32 pages, they are not the right hire.**

Supporting characters (Mother, Max, Lily, etc.) must be described and approved in their own reference notes before illustration begins. Do not let the illustrator invent these characters — provide reference direction.

## SETTINGS — WHERE THESE STORIES LIVE

All three books are grounded in **Atlanta, Georgia**. The settings are:

**Inside the Sanders household:**
- David's bedroom (appears in *Silent Prayer* — the franchise's most important interior setting)
  - Warm amber nightlight near the floor
  - Moonlight at the window
  - Bookshelf with well-read books
  - Desk with notebook and pen
  - Everything lived-in, not staged
- The family kitchen (warm ochre-amber walls, wooden cabinets, a beam of morning light from the kitchen window — same beam that appears on the website and in the adventure series)
- The family porch (outdoor — appears in *Silent Prayer* — where David sits to think at night; the Atlanta sky visible, rooftops and streetlights in background)

**Outside the household:**
- An Atlanta neighborhood street and park (appears in multiple books)
- A school classroom (appears in *Mirror Reflections*)

**The rule for all settings:** Specific, lived-in, Atlanta-warm. No generic pastoral landscapes. No mountains. No "anywhere USA." This is a Black American household in Atlanta, and the visual world should reflect that with love and specificity.

## BOOK 1: SILENT PRAYER — SUMMARY FOR ILLUSTRATOR

**Story in one sentence:** David tries to pray one night and the words won't come — he sits with his mother, then goes to the porch alone, and discovers that silence can be its own kind of prayer.

**7 spreads:**
1. David kneeling in his bedroom — the habit of prayer, warm and easy
2. David sitting on the edge of his bed — words won't come, something feels off
3. Close on David's hands in his lap — the fear interior
4. Mother sits beside him, one hand on his back — one line of truth delivered
5. The porch at night — Atlanta sky, David looking up, something opening
6. Back in the bedroom — eyes closed, hands open, stillness chosen
7. David sleeping — the room dim and warm, peace

**Easter egg (important for illustrator):** On the final spread, there is a small figurine on the nightstand — a small white shape. Readers who know Angel will recognize it. First-time readers see only a toy. Do not label it or make it obvious. It is a quiet detail.

**Full spread-by-spread brief:** See `LILDEV-SILENT-PRAYER.md` for detailed scene descriptions, emotional notes, and light direction for every spread.

When submitting to Amazon KDP, the illustrator or Jerry will need:
- PDF/X-1a compliance for print
- 0.125" bleed on all sides
- Cover file as separate from interior pages

## HOW TO FIND AN ILLUSTRATOR

Best platforms:
- **Publishing Crawler** (publishingcrawler.com) — specifically for book illustrators
- **Behance** — search "children's book illustration"
- **Instagram** — hashtags: #childrensbookillustrator #picturebook #kidsbook
- **Upwork** — post a job listing
- **The Society of Children's Book Writers and Illustrators** (scbwi.org) — professional organization, find members who illustrate

Ask for:
1. 3 samples of completed picture book work (not single illustrations — full books or spreads)
2. Their rate for 32-page picture book (all-rights, work-for-hire)
3. Their turnaround time
4. Experience illustrating Black characters specifically — this is not optional, ask directly

---

## WHAT MAKES THE RIGHT ILLUSTRATOR

- They can maintain character consistency across 32+ pages without drift
- Their portfolio shows warmth — not just technical skill
- Their line work is clean and bold, not sketchy or loose
- They can do flat cel style (not every illustrator works this way — confirm before hiring)
- They have illustrated Black children before and their work shows care, not stereotypes
- They communicate clearly and hit their deadlines

---

## BEFORE YOU HIRE — THE PAID TEST

Before committing to the full book, ask the illustrator to do one thing first:

> *"Please illustrate Spread 5 of Silent Prayer — the porch scene. David sits on the Atlanta porch steps at night, looking up at the sky. Reference character sheets will be provided. Use the style described in the brief."*

Pay them fairly for this test ($100–$300 depending on level). This one spread will tell you everything you need to know about whether they can deliver the whole book.
