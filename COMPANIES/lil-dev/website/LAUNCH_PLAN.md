## STEP 3 — Find and Commission the Character Artist (Week 1–2)

This is the most important step in the entire plan. Do not skip it or delay it.

**Who does it:** Jerry (find + hire) + Claude (writes the brief)

### Where to Find Artists

Look on these platforms. Search: *children's book illustration*, *character design kids*, *cartoon character sheet*

- **Behance** (behance.net) — portfolio site, DM artists directly
- **ArtStation** (artstation.com) — professional portfolios
- **Instagram** — search hashtags: #kidsbookillustrator #characterdesign #childrensillustration
- **Fiverr** (fiverr.com) — search: *children character design* — sort by Top Rated
- **Upwork** (upwork.com) — post a job listing

### What to Look For in an Artist

- Their style should feel **warm, expressive, and modern** — think Bluey, Craig of the Creek, or Kipo
- They must have a portfolio showing **character sheets** (front view, side view, expressions)
- They should have experience with **children's or animation-style characters**, not just realistic art
- They should be able to deliver: character turnaround sheets + expression sheets + color palette

### Budget Ranges (Real Numbers)

| Level | Price Range | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Entry / emerging | $150–$400 per character | Good quality, slower revisions |
| Mid-level | $400–$900 per character | Strong work, responsive |
| Professional | $900–$2,500 per character | Animation-ready quality |

For launch, you need: **Lil Dev** (full sheet) + **Angel** (full sheet) + any 1-2 supporting characters. Budget $500–$1,500 minimum for quality that will hold up across merch, books, and animation.

### Ask Claude to Write the Brief

Before you contact any artist, say to Claude:

> *"Write me the character commission brief for Lil Dev and Angel"*

Claude will produce a full document you can send to any artist that describes: the character look, personality, colors, expressions needed, and what files you need delivered.

### When You Find Someone You Like

1. Send them the brief Claude wrote
2. Ask for a **rough sketch of one expression** (most serious artists charge $0–$50 for this; some do it free to win the job)
3. Review the sketch — does it feel like Lil Dev?
4. If yes, hire them and pay the deposit (usually 50% upfront, 50% on delivery)
5. Expect 4–8 weeks for full delivery with revisions

# THE ANIMATED SERIES — THE LONG GAME

**Don't try to produce a full animated series before launch.** Here's the correct order:

| Stage | When | What It Takes |
|---|---|---|
| Launch trailer (60 sec) | Before hard launch | $300–$1,500, 4–8 weeks |
| Animated short (5 min) | 3–6 months after launch | $5,000–$15,000, 8–16 weeks |
| Full episode (11 min) | After short lands well | $15,000–$50,000 per episode |
| Full series | After audience exists | Co-production deal or crowdfund |

The short is what you use to pitch streaming platforms, apply for grants, or run a crowdfund (Kickstarter). You need an audience before the short will mean anything to anyone. Build the audience, then make the short, then pitch the series.
