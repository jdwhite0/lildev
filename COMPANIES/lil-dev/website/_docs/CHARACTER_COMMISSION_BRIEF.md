# LIL DEV — CHARACTER COMMISSION BRIEF
*For artists being commissioned to design the official Lil Dev World characters*
*Send this document to the artist when you reach out*

---

## WHO THIS IS FOR

You are the artist being hired to create the **official, final, production-ready character designs** for Lil Dev World — an original children's entertainment franchise. These designs will be used across books, merchandise, animation, marketing, and all franchise products. They need to last 20+ years.

This is not a one-off illustration job. This is the foundation of a franchise.

---

## THE PROJECT IN PLAIN LANGUAGE

**What is Lil Dev World?**
A children's entertainment franchise in the tradition of Bluey, Craig of the Creek, and Avatar: The Last Airbender. It centers on an 8-year-old Black boy named David Sanders (nicknamed "Lil Dev") and his dog Angel as they explore their Atlanta neighborhood, ask big questions, and grow through every adventure. Target audience: kids ages 3–12. Tone: warm, curious, faith-grounded, aspirational.

**Why does this commission matter?**
Every book illustration, every piece of merchandise, every future animated episode depends on the character designs being locked, consistent, and expressive. Nothing else in the franchise can be produced until these designs exist.

## ART STYLE — THIS IS CRITICAL

### Style Target

**2D flat cel cartoon with Pixar-quality expressiveness.**

This means:
- **Bold outlines** — characters read cleanly against any background
- **Flat color fills** — no volume shading on the characters themselves
- **Expressive anatomy** — the style is not stiff or clipart-simple. The bodies are fluid and readable even in complex poses
- **Clean, consistent line weight** — outlines should be consistent throughout the character sheet

What it is NOT:
- Not 3D rendered / Pixar 3D
- Not realistic illustration
- Not minimalist/flat icon style
- Not the hyper-stylized angular look of some modern cartoons

**The test:** Does it feel like it belongs in a world that also has Bluey and Craig of the Creek? If yes, you're in the right zone.

---

### Style References (Search These)

Study these specifically for tone, expression quality, and line work:

- **Miles Morales (Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse)** — cultural energy, expressive face, body language
- **Craig of the Creek (Cartoon Network)** — a real Black kid, realistic everyday energy, warm palette
- **Bluey (ABC Kids)** — warmth, family-centered, emotionally intelligent character design
- **Avatar: The Last Airbender** — expressive anatomy, emotional range, clean line work

Do NOT reference:
- Superhero muscular proportions
- Overly "cute"/baby proportions
- Highly angular/geometric character design
- Anime proportions
